The Philippines, boasting over 7,000 islands, is the ultimate destination for a one-of-a-kind getaway. This tropical paradise boasts an abundance of natural wonders, a diverse cultural heritage, and a fun-filled atmosphere that is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors. With so many islands to explore, there is something for everyone, whether you seek thrilling pursuits, breathtaking landscapes, or unforgettable encounters with locals.

Below are six popular landmarks that you can find here in the Philippines:

Chocolate Hills, Bohol

The Chocolate Hills of Bohol, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Natural Monument, is a geomorphic marvel that any visitor to the Philippines must see. With 1,268 cone-shaped hills that turn a beautiful chocolatey brown during the dry season, the hills are truly a stunning sight to behold. The Chocolate Hills are a must-visit destination for any trip to Bohol and are an iconic landmark of the Philippines.

Nacpan Beach, El Nido

El Nido, in Palawan, is a paradise island that is renowned for its pristine beaches and clear lagoons. However, there is another idyllic destination only 45 minutes away from the main town: Nacpan Beach. It is a must-see for island-hopping escapades in El Nido, including a visit to El Nido Big Lagoon and other top Palawan beaches. A trip to Nacpan Beach after a day filled with island-hiding adventures is highly recommended as it's the perfect spot to unwind and relax.

Cloud 9, Siargao

Siargao in the Philippines has become a hotspot for surfers from across the globe, with Cloud 9 being the most famous and challenging destination for experienced surfers. It is a staple on any itinerary for visitors to Siargao. For the more adventurous or advanced surfers, Cloud 9 is a must-visit spot to test their skills and see how they fare in one of the best surfing conditions in Asia.
