The Philippines, boasting over 7,000 islands, is the ultimate destination for a one-of-a-kind getaway. This tropical paradise boasts an abundance of natural wonders, a diverse cultural heritage, and a fun-filled atmosphere that is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors. With so many islands to explore, there is something for everyone, whether you seek thrilling pursuits, breathtaking landscapes, or unforgettable encounters with locals.

Below are six popular landmarks that you can find here in the Philippines:

Tubbataha Reef, Palawan

Tubbataha Reef is a must-visit for divers and marine conservationists in the Philippines. Accessible only by liveaboard vessel from Puerto Princesa, it's a challenging but rewarding destination known for its marine biodiversity and unique underwater life. The Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and was on the shortlist for the New Seven Wonders of the Natural World.

Kawasan Falls, Cebu

Kawasan Falls in Cebu is a must-visit spot for anyone looking to cool off in turquoise waters. Its multi-layered waterfalls and 40-meter height offer a unique swimming experience, especially on bamboo rafts. The 10-minute trek leads to a quieter second cascade, near the nearby town of Moalboal that offers its own unique sardine run experience.

Mayon Volcano, Albay, Bicol

The majestic and active Mayon Volcano, the most perfect volcanic cone in the world, towers over the Bicol landscape, greeting visitors with its imposing beauty. It attracts tourists from all over the world, who come to admire its striking features and marvel at its incredible natural splendor. With its impressive height and perfect conical shape, Mayon Volcano is truly one of the most beautiful natural wonders of the Philippines.
